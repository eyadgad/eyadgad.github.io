1.1 Problem 1


Omani wants to develop a grade management system for his students to store their records. He has a list of records and each record consists of a student's name, course name, and associated grades. The student's name and course name are strings, while the grades are represented as a tuple containing coursework and final exam scores, with their total not exceeding 100.
For example, a record might look like ['Sara', 'English', (60, 40)], indicating Sara got full marks in both coursework and the final exam for the English course. As students may enroll in multiple courses, their names could appear multiple times in list of records. For example, if Sara also takes a French course and receives grades (35, 38),there would be two records for Sara in the list.

Omani wants to transform this list of records into a new formatted list where each element is a list represents a unique student along with their enrolled courses and corresponding grades. For example, Sara's consolidated record would be ['Sara', ['English', 'French'], [(60, 40), (35, 38)]]. In this format, the first element is the student's name, the second element lists the courses taken, and the third element contains tuples representing the grades for each respective course.


Notes

  • Each course has a coursework grade out of 60 marks and a final exam grade out of 40 marks.
  • The final grade for a course is the sum of its coursework and final exam grades, totaling 100 marks.

Standard Input

  • A single integer number representing the number of records in the list.
  • Multiple lines, each line contains a single list representing single record.

    • Standard Output

  • Multiple lines, each line contains a single list representing a student along with their courses and corresponding grades.

    • 1.3 Problem 3


    In the quest to foster an equitable grading system and ensure a high standard of education, Omani has introduced a universal passing criterion applicable across all courses. According to this new regulation, a student will successfully pass a course only if they achieve a minimum of 60% in coursework and a minimum of 50% in the final exam. Omani now seeks to integrate this universal passing criterion into the existing grade management system to automatically determine the pass or fail status for each student based on the transformed records where each record represents a unique student along with their courses and corresponding grades.

    For example, let's consider Sara's courses and their respective grades. After applying the universal passing criterion, it is determined that Sara will pass in English and Physics, while she will fail in French and Math. This decision is based on the fact that the coursework grade for French does not reach the minimum requirement of 60%, and the final exam grade for Math does not reach the minimum requirement of 50%.


    Notes

    • 60% in coursework equates to 36 marks out of 60.
    • 50% in the final exam corresponds to 20 marks out of 40.
